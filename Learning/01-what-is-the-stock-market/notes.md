# My Initial Understanding

**Before studying the topic, I understand that the stock market is :**

The stock market is a place where people can buy and sell small ownership stakes in companies, known as shares or stocks. By purchasing a share, an investor becomes a partial owner of that company.

I think people buy stocks because they believe the company will become more valuable in the future. If that happens, the stock price increases, allowing investors to make a profit if they sell their shares for more than they originally paid. However, if the company performs poorly or people lose confidence in its future, the stock price may fall, causing investors to lose money.

I also think companies enter the stock market to raise money. Instead of borrowing from a bank and repaying a loan with interest, a company can sell some of its ownership to investors in exchange for capital. The company receives money that it can use to expand its business, while investors receive shares representing partial ownership.

Overall, I currently think the stock market connects companies that need money with investors who want to grow their wealth.

## Things I Think I Understand

- A stock (or share) represents partial ownership of a company.
- Companies can sell shares to raise money instead of relying entirely on bank loans.
- Investors hope to earn money by buying shares that increase in value over time.
- Stock prices can rise and fall depending on how well a company performs and how investors view its future.
- People can buy and sell shares through the stock market.

## My Current Mental Model

**1.** A company wants to grow but needs more money.

**2.** Instead of taking a loan, it sells part of its ownership by issuing shares.

**3.** Investors buy those shares and become partial owners of the company.

**4.** The company receives money from those investors to fund its operations or expansion.

**5.** Afterward, investors can buy and sell those shares with other investors through the stock market.

**6.** If the company becomes more successful or investors believe it has a strong future, the share price increases. If the opposite happens, the share price decreases.

**7.** Investors make money if they sell their shares for more than they paid, and lose money if they sell for less.
